Why you should adopt an Indian Pariah Dog?

Before knowing why to adopt an Indian pariah dog one must know what is actually an Indian pariah dog. Indian pariah dog has many names such as South Asian dog or the desi dog, desi kutta or Pye dog. Dogs which are mostly seen on the streets in India belong to the Indian pye dog breed. They have erect ears, a wedge-shaped head, and a curved tail. These dogs are also used as police dogs and guard dogs as they are easy to be trained.

Indian pariah dogs originally belong to the Indian subcontinent. They have short coat and a lifespan of about 10 to 13 years. Both the male and female dogs are different from each other in their weights and heights . The males usually weigh around 15 to 35 kilograms in the female weigh almost around 15 to 25 kilograms. And the height of the males is around 20 to 25 in and similarly for the females it is 18 to 23 in. The most commonly observed colours are browns, ranging from dark to reddish-brown, with or without white markings. Solid blacks are rare, but some dogs are pied. Shaded coats, brindles, solid white and dalmatian-type spotting are never seen in pure populations. These may be a sign of mixing with modern breeds, as they are only seen in dogs in cities and other sites where non-native dogs have been introduced.

So now, why should one adopt an Indian pariah dog? Being a naturally evolved breed, they have very few health concerns and thrive with minimal maintenance in suitable climates. They adjust well in tropical climates as there is a mix of both winters and summers. But, even extreme weather conditions do not affect them in any way. They don't need much cleaning and grooming 'cause they are adapted to clean themselves on their own and also they have little body odour. Genetic health ailments like hip dysplasia are extremely rare, since there is no inbreeding and the dominant genes that aid their survival are naturally selected over time. Most of their deaths occur due to accidents on the roads and railway tracks, not getting food or drinking polluted water, tumors in the body, getting bit by snakes or being beaten up by humans.

So it becomes very convenient for the adoptive individual to raise an Indian pariah dog because they require less grooming and are also not prone to much diseases. It just costs about Rs 500/- to Rs. 1000/- to give a good life to an Indian pariah dog . In return the adoptive parent gets a best friend who will love unconditionally for his entire life and be a loyal pet. The only thing they ask is sufficient food, shelter, love and care from their adoptive parent.
